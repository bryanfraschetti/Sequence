Sequence

Get the most out of Spotify's unique crossfading feature and rearrange your playlists to flow based on key signature and tempo

How it works

Upon logging in, this web app connects to your account data in the Spotify API, allowing the application access to your playlists and the tracks therein. Having access to the tracks in your playlist allows us to collect relevant data on the songs via the Audio Analysis endpoint. Moreover, this endpoint has data on the key signature, duration, BPM, timbre and pretty much anything you could imaginably want to know about a track. When you select one of your playlists, it loads the data from each of the songs and presents you with a variety of ways to organize your playlist. From which, your selection invokes a sorting algorithm that rearranges the tracks by the relevant metrics. The new sequence then manifests in Spotify as a playlist with the same name as the initial playlist with an additional bit of text pertaining to which organizing method you selected. More specifics are available in the about section.

How it works

When you click on the "Get Started" button you will be redirected to a Spotify login page asking you to grant certain permissions. The permissions required are for us to:

  • View your spotify data such as your name and username, your profile picture, how many followers you have on Spotify and your public playlists.
  • View the content you are playing
  • Create, edit, and follow private playlists Create, edit, and follow playlists

Addressing these in a different order than presented, firstly we need to access your private and public playlists in order to view the songs on your playlist. We access this data via this endpoint. As it says, it shows us the playlists you own and follow. Sometimes, private playlists and followed playlists (strangely) do not show up when they are requested. The best practice for using this app is to own the playlist and make the playlist that you would like to resequence public; after reordering you can always revert this and make it private of course.

We request the content you are currently playing as we are planning on integrating some features in the future that will make use of this.

Lastly we need to access your user information for your spotify userid. This allows us to create a new playlist via this endpoint, which requires a userid (to know where to put the playlist). Since this is modifying a playlist, we also need to have the permissions to "create, edit, and follow public/private playlists"

Knowing how much music matters, we would never want to jeopardize your playlists and so we never modify your playlists directly. Instead, we create a new playlist that has the same name as the playlist that you select to reorganize. In order to distinguish, we add a suffix phrase related to the organizational method selected. For example if your playlist is called "Chill Music," and you organize by the circle of fifths you will see a new playlist on spotify "Chill Music COF Sequenced."
